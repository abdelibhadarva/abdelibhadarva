Know Your Regulatory Bodies in Ayurveda

Orientation Module for BAMS Transitional Curriculum

Why Know Your Regulators?

As a BAMS student, you are entering a structured system of professional education. Understanding the bodies that govern this system is crucial for your academic journey and future career.

  • It clarifies the framework of your education and examinations.
  • You will understand how your college gets permission and is inspected.
  • It prepares you for future registration as a practitioner.
  • You learn about the bodies that fund and promote Ayurvedic research.
  • It empowers you to be an informed and compliant professional.

The Ministry of AYUSH (MoA)

  • Established: 9th November 2014, by elevating the Dept. of AYUSH.
  • Purpose: To ensure the optimal development and propagation of AYUSH systems of healthcare.
  • Role: The apex body for policy formulation, development, research, and regulation of Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homoeopathy.

Aim & Vision (2025)

  • Aim: To promote and mainstream AYUSH systems in national healthcare.
  • Vision 2025: "To establish AYUSH systems as the preferred choice of holistic healthcare for the people, integrating them into the national healthcare system, and ensuring global acceptance."

Deep Dive: NCISM

The National Commission for Indian System of Medicine is the most important body for you as a student. It replaced the old CCIM in 2021.

  • Established: 2021 (under the NCISM Act, 2020)
  • Headquarters: New Delhi
  • Chairperson (2025): Vaidya Jayant Deopujari

Major Functions of NCISM

  • Sets and maintains standards for education (syllabi, college infrastructure).
  • Grants recognition to Ayurvedic colleges and qualifications.
  • Conducts inspections and assessments of colleges.
  • Maintains the National Register of practitioners.
  • Regulates professional conduct and ethics.

University vs. NCISM: Role Difference

NCISM

Grants RECOGNITION to your college and qualification. Sets the curriculum. Maintains the central register.

Your University

Grants the DEGREE (e.g., BAMS). Conducts examinations. Handles affiliation and academic administration.

The Vice-Chancellor

The Vice-Chancellor (VC) is the head of your university (e.g., Gujarat Ayurved University).

Key Functions:

  • Principal academic and administrative officer of the university.
  • Chairs key university bodies (like the Academic Council).
  • Responsible for implementing university policies.
  • Confers degrees during convocation.

Deep Dive: CCRAS

The Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences is the apex body for formulating, coordinating, and promoting research in Ayurveda.

  • Mandate: Scientific validation of Ayurveda, drug development, and literary research.
  • Headquarters: New Delhi
  • Director General (2025): Prof. (Vd.) Rabinarayan Acharya

Core Functions of CCRAS

  • Conducts clinical, pharmacological, and literary research.
  • Standardizes Ayurvedic drugs and formulations.
  • Collaborates with national and international research institutions.
  • Runs training programs for researchers and practitioners.
  • Publishes research journals and monographs.

Post-Internship Registration

After your 1-year internship, you must register to practice legally. Here is the typical process:

  1. Complete your mandatory rotating internship and get the completion certificate from your college.
  2. Gather documents: BAMS degrees, internship certificate, identity proof, photos.
  3. Apply to your State Ayurveda Council/Board (e.g., Gujarat Board of Ayurvedic & Unani Systems of Medicine).
  4. Pay the required fee and complete any verification.
  5. Receive your State Registration Number.
  6. Your state registration is automatically updated to the National Register maintained by NCISM.

Research & Global Collaborations

Ayurveda is gaining global recognition through strategic collaborations:

  • WHO Collaborating Centres (CCs): MoA has established WHO CCs for traditional medicine in India (e.g., at ITRA, Jamnagar).
  • ICMR/CSIR Tie-ups: Collaborative research projects with top national science bodies to validate Ayurvedic principles.
  • International MOUs: Agreements with universities and countries for research and education in Ayurveda.

Summary / Take-Home Message

  • The Ministry of AYUSH is the apex body guiding policy.
  • NCISM is your primary regulator: It designs your syllabus, inspects your college, and will register you as a doctor.
  • CCRAS is the primary research body, working to scientifically validate and develop Ayurveda.
  • Your University conducts exams and awards your BAMS degree.
  • Your career is built on this foundation of regulation, education, and research.
